Utan: Visayan Soup

Utan Bisaya is a simple yet nutritious Filipino vegetable soup originating from the Visayas region. It's a light, flavorful dish packed with vitamins and minerals. While recipes vary, the core ingredients and method remain consistent.

Ingredients:


1 cup cubed squash
1 cup sliced okra
1 cup coconut cream
1 cup sliced eggplant
1 cup cut into pieces string beans
4 cups water
1-2 tablespoons crushed ginger
1-2 stalks lemongrass (optional)
Fish sauce
Salt
1 sachet/cube maggi magic sarap

Procedure:


1. Prepare Vegetables: Wash and chop all vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
2. Fry Fish (optional): If using fish, fry it until golden brown and set aside to flake later.
3. Boil Vegetables: Bring water to a boil in a pot. Add ginger and lemongrass (if using). Simmer for 5 minutes to extract flavor.
4. Add Vegetables: Add the harder vegetables (like squash and taro) first. Simmer for 5-7 minutes. Add the remaining vegetables, except the leafy greens, and simmer for another 3-5 minutes.
5. Season: Add fish sauce, salt, and bouillon cube (if using). Stir well.
6. Add Fish (optional): Add flaked fried fish.
7. Add Leafy Greens: Add leafy greens and simmer for 1-2 minutes until wilted.
8. Serve: Serve hot, ideally with steamed rice and fried fish (if not already included).
Nutrition Facts per 1 cup serving
Nutrients:
Calories 160 kcal
Protein 7 g
Fat 1 g
Carbs 37 g
Sodium 282 mg
