La Paz Batchoy

La Paz Batchoy, originating from La Paz Public Market in Iloilo City, Philippines, is a rich and savory noodle soup. While recipes vary, the core elements remain consistent: a flavorful broth, tender pork and organ meats, fresh noodles, and crunchy toppings. The broth is crucial. Many recipes call for a long simmering of pork and beef bones to extract maximum flavor. Some recipes also include chicken stock.

Ingredients:


1 kg Pork bones (beef bones optional)
250g Pork belly
150g Pork liver
150g Pork intestines (optional, cleaned)
150g Chicken breast (optional)
150g Shrimp (optional)
500g Miki noodles
6-8 cups Water
1 large, minced onion
4-6 cloves, minced garlic
1 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
2 tbsp worcestershire sauce
1 tsp soy sauce
1 tbsp sugar
salt and paper to taste
1/2 crushed chicharon
2 tbsp fried garlic
chopped green onions for garnish
1 raw egg per bowl (optional)

Procedure:


1. Broth: Simmer pork and beef bones in water for at least 2 hours, skimming off impurities. Strain the broth
2. Meat: Add pork belly, intestines (if using), and chicken (if using) to the broth. Simmer until tender (about 30-45 minutes). Add shrimp (if using) during the last few minutes. Remove meats and slice.
3. Liver: Saut√© the liver separately until cooked. Slice.
4. Seasoning: Add minced onion, garlic, shrimp paste, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and sugar to the broth. Simmer for 15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Noodles: Cook miki noodles according to package directions
6. Assembly: Place noodles in bowls. Top with sliced pork, liver, and other meats. Ladle hot broth over the noodles. Garnish with chicharon, fried garlic, and green onions. Add a raw egg (if desired). Serve immediately.
