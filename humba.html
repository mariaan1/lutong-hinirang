Humba

Humba is a beloved Filipino braised pork dish, particularly popular in the Visayas and Mindanao regions. Its characteristic sweet and savory flavor profile comes from a rich sauce typically made with soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and aromatics. Many variations exist, reflecting regional preferences and family traditions. This recipe offers a common approach.

Ingredients:


1 kg pork belly, cut into serving pieces (Pork shoulder, hocks, or a combination can also be used)
1/4 cup soy sauce
1/4 cup vinegar (cane or coconut vinegar is traditional, but rice vinegar works)
1 medium onion, minced
5 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup lemon-lime soda (Sprite or 7-Up are commonly used; adds sweetness and tenderness)
1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
1/4 cup dried banana blossoms (optional, adds a unique flavor and texture)
1/4 cup salted black beans (tausi), drained
3-5 bay leaves
2 star anise (optional)
Water (as needed)

Procedure:


1. Render the Fat: Place the pork belly in a pan over medium heat. Cook until some of the fat renders, creating oil. Remove the pork and set aside. Reserve about a tablespoon of the rendered fat in the pan.
2. Sauté Aromatics: Add the minced onion and garlic to the pan and sauté until lightly browned. Return the pork to the pan.
3. Combine Ingredients: Add the black beans, soy sauce, and vinegar. Simmer for 3 minutes without stirring to allow the flavors to meld. Then, gently stir in the peppercorns, bay leaves, and star anise (if using).
4. Braising: Add the lemon-lime soda and enough water to almost cover the pork. Bring to a simmer, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the pork is very tender.
5. Finish: Add the banana blossoms (if using) during the last 5 minutes of cooking. Simmer until the sauce thickens slightly.
6. Serve: Serve hot, ideally with steamed rice.
