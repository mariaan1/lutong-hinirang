Visayas

Visayan Islands, island group, central Philippines. The Visayan group consists of seven large and several hundred smaller islands clustered around the Visayan, Samar, and Camotes seas. The seven main islands are Bohol, Cebu, Leyte, Masbate, Negros, Panay, and Samar. These islands and their smaller neighbours make up the central group of the Philippine archipelago.

- Britannica 2025

Kinilaw

Kinilaw, a Filipino ceviche, features fresh seafood "cooked" in an acidic marinade. The key is using extremely fresh, high-quality seafood. Many variations exist, but the core ingredients and steps are consistent.

La Paz Batchoy

La Paz Batchoy, originating from La Paz Public Market in Iloilo City, Philippines, is a rich and savory noodle soup. While recipes vary, the core elements remain consistent: a flavorful broth, tender pork and organ meats, fresh noodles, and crunchy toppings.


Utan

Utan Bisaya is a simple yet nutritious Filipino vegetable soup originating from the Visayas region. It's a light, flavorful dish packed with vitamins and minerals. While recipes vary, the core ingredients and method remain consistent.


Chicken Inasal

Chicken Inasal is a beloved Filipino dish, particularly popular in the Visayas region. It's characterized by its distinctive marinade and grilling method, resulting in juicy, flavorful chicken with a vibrant orange hue.


Humba

Humba is a beloved Filipino braised pork dish, particularly popular in the Visayas and Mindanao regions. Its characteristic sweet and savory flavor profile comes from a rich sauce typically made with soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and aromatics. Many variations exist, reflecting regional preferences and family traditions. This recipe offers a common approach.
