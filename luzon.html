Luzon

Luzon, largest and most important island of the Philippines. It is the site of Manila, the nations capital and major metropolis, and of Quezon City. Located on the northern part of the Philippine archipelago, it is bounded by the Philippine Sea (east), Sibuyan Sea (south), and the South China Sea (west). To the north, the Luzon Strait separates Luzon from Taiwan.

- Britannica 2025

~ ~ Popular Recipes ~ ~


Adobo

Adobo is considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines. It was a marinating and cooking meat with vinegar and salt that was later influenced by Spanish colonization, introducing soy sauce and other spices.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a traditional Filipino sour soup, often made with tamarind. It's loved for its sour flavor and its good with meats and seafood.

Bicol Express

Originating from the Bicol region, famous for its love of spicy foods, Bicol Express is a creamy, and spicy dish made with coconut milk and chilies.

Sisig

Sisig is a Kapampangan dish made traditionally from pigs head parts and liver, seasoned and served sizzling. It is now a famous Filipino pulutan and main dish.

Igado

Igado is a popular Ilocano dish composed of pork meat and liver stewed with vinegar, soy sauce, and vegetables. It's a reflection of Spanish influence name from "higado," Spanish for "liver".
