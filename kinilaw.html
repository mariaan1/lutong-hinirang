Kinilaw

Kinilaw, a Filipino ceviche, features fresh seafood "cooked" in an acidic marinade. The key is using extremely fresh, high-quality seafood. Many variations exist, but the core ingredients and steps are consistent.

Ingredients:


1 lb/450g Seafood: Tuna (tanigue, yellowfin), mackerel (tulingan), marlin, or other sashimigrade fish
½ to ¾ cup vinegar and ¼ to ½ cup citrus juice.
Aromatics & Spices
1-2 tbsp Minced ginger
½ to 1 medium Thinly sliced red onion
Chopped chili peppers
Ground black pepper (to taste)
Salt (to taste)
Diced tomatoes (optional)
Chopped green onions (optional)
Coconut milk or cream (optional)
Green mangoes (optional)

Procedure:


1. Prepare Seafood: Clean and cube the seafood into 1-inch pieces.
2. Marinate: In a non-reactive bowl, combine vinegar and citrus juice. Add seafood, ensuring it's fully submerged. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 15-30 minutes (longer for a firmer texture). The fish will become opaque as it "cooks."
3. Add Aromatics: Drain excess liquid. Add ginger, onion, chilies, pepper, and salt. Gently toss to combine.
4. Adjust the marinating time and spice levels to your preference.
5. Chill & Serve: Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours (or longer for a more intense flavor). Serve chilled, garnished as desired.
Nutrition Facts per 1 cup serving
Nutrients:
Calories 93.4 kcal
Protein 17.8 g
Fat 1.3 g
Carbs 2.7 g
Sodium 349.4 mg
